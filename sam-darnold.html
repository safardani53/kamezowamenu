Sam Darnold | Early Life

Sam was born on June 5, 1997, to his parents, Michael Darnold and Chris Darnold, in Dane Point, California.

He then watched ESPN with his Father. He began playing basketball when he was just five.

As a child, Sam used to play different sports, such as basketball, baseball, soccer, volleyball, and taekwondo.

Family Circle

Undoubtedly, Sam was brought up in an entirely athletic family. Sam’s Father, Michael, served as an offensive lineman at the University of Redlands in California.

Sam’s mother, Chris, played volleyball at Long Beach City College. Currently, she serves as a physical education teacher at Shorecliffs Middle School.

Sam Darnold | High School Career

Sam went to San Clemente High School in California. For his first year, he played baseball and later played both basketball and football.

He played basketball exceptionally well, leading him to win South Coast League Most Valuable Player twice. He was also titled to the California Interscholastic Federation team initially.

According to his basketball coach Marc Popovich, his basketball skills played the main role in turning him into a DEFENSIVE FOOTBALL player. 

Sam was touched by the opportunity to play as a college basketball player in Pac-12 Conference or Mountain West Conference.

As a sophomore, Sam was a swingman in varsity basketball and a starting linebacker/wide receiver for varsity football teams.

He got to play a quarterback first as his team’s starting quarterback was injured. 

He became an alleged quarterback when he broke the record for the most touchdown passes.

Sam could not play spontaneously in his junior year as he suffered a foot injury. He successfully participated in the CIF-Southern Section Southwest Division championship game for his senior year to lead his team to a 12-2 record.

Sam summed up his high school career with 3,000 passing yards and 39 touchdowns, along with 800 rushing yards and 13 touchdowns.

Rivals.com, a four-star recruit, also rated him the eighth-best dual-threat quarterback in his class and the 179th-best player.

Sam Darnold | College Career

After observing his multi-sport personality, many universities, such as Oregon, Utah, Northwestern, and Duke, offered him scholarships.

Further, Clay Helton and Steve Sarkisian, USC coaches, became a helping hand and led him to a scholarship to play for the Trojans.

Well, for the first year at college, Sam was redshirted. His first game was against the Utah Utes but unfortunately lost to it.

He conquered it all when he captured five touchdown passes in consecutive games and became the first quarterback in college history to do so.

Sam was entitled to the Freshman Offensive Player of the Year in the 2016 Pac-12 Conference.

Not only that, he participated in the 2017 Rose Bowl for USC. He was honored with the  Archie Griffin Award and was entitled to the Freshman All-America team.

Moreover, Sam became an early favorite for the Heisman Trophy. He also led his team to win over the 2017 Stanford Cardinal team, Texas Longhorns. 

He headed his team to the Pac-12 Conference championship and earned a final position in the 2017 Cotton Bowl.

His stats were fascinating in college football:57 touchdowns and 3000 passing yards, seven touchdowns, and 332 rushing yards.

Sam Darnold | Professional Career

The New York Jets drafted him in the first round, with the third overall pick of the 2018 NFL draft. Sam agreed to a four-year contract worth $30.25 million.

Not to mention, Sam was entitled the starter for Week 1 when he played in the first preseason game against the Atlanta Falcons and led to a 17-0 win.

Along with his team, he faced tough competitions against the Detroit Lions, the Miami Dolphins, Jacksonville Jaguars, and Denver Broncos.

Sam missed three games due to a foot injury but returned to action in Week 14 against the Buffalo Bills.

He played for the Jets from 2018-2020, where he played 38 games and started all of them.

During his time in the Jets, his stats were; 8097 yards and 54 passing touchdowns, and 417 yards and 5 rushing touchdowns.

In April 2021, Sam was traded to Carolina Panthers for a guaranteed $18.85 million for the 2022 season.

For the Carolina Panthers, he played for 12 games, starting in 11 of them, before suffering an ankle injury ruling him out for 4-6 weeks.

So far, for the Panthers, he has 2527 yards and 9 passing touchdowns, 222 yards, and 5 rushing touchdowns.
