Rich Gannon | High School Career

Rich went to Saint Joseph’s Preparatory School in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, and nourished himself more as an athlete.

He retrieved three varsity letters both in football and rowing. He also earned two varsity letters in basketball as a senior year student.

Furthermore, he grabbed the first-team All-City award as a quarterback and a punter, throwing 1567 yards in his senior year.

Rich Gannon | College Career

For attending college, Rich went to the University of Delaware, where he took the membership of Sigma Phi Epsilon.

Rich Gannon | Professional Career

Minnesota Vikings

Rich did not really enter the NFL with a silver spoon in his mouth. He had to strive really hard for it. And all those awards and achievements received in college depict his ard work. We don’t need to clarify again and again.

As a result, Rich was soon hired by the  New England Patriots with 98th overall pickup in the fourth round of the 1987 NFL Draft and placed into a running back position.

He could not spend much time with the Patriots as he was soon waived to the Minnesota Vikings.

And, that was the strong time when he started as a quarterback. Rich played six regular seasons for the Vikings and led his team to win over the Atlanta Falcons, the New Orleans Saints, the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, Detroit Lions in 1987-1988.

Despite all these victories, somewhere, he lagged to win the  NFC championship game in 1987. Rich played along with Kramer and Wilson as a quarterback for the Vikings.

For the first time in his career, Rich was spotted with an injury in 1992 where he hyperextended his left knee while gaining a victory over the Lions. The winning and losing stories remained on their way for Rich until 1992.

Washington Redskins

Rich moved to the Washington Redskins in 1994. He was positioned as the third-stringer, supporting starter Mark Rypien and backup Cary Conklin. He presented himself in eight games and initiated four games.

During weeks 6-7, he faced a foot injury and had to leave his position for a few games. For the 1994 season, he was again benched out because of a shoulder injury.

Kansas City Chiefs

Rich inked a deal with the Kansas City Chiefs in 1995.  He was placed as a backup to Steve Bono.

Things went into turmoil when there held a quarterback in 1997 because Rich led the team in Elvis Grbac’s absence. As a result, the team and Rich had to part ways in 1998.

Oakland Raiders

Rich was appointed as a free agent by the  Oakland Raiders in February 1999. He then soon remained victorious enough to seize the Pro Bowl MVP award regularly for the years 2001 and 2002.

His winning stories do not only get stuck to that. There’s more coming. Rich received the NFL Most Valuable Player Award in 2002. While other players still dream of it, he conquered it.

Not to mention, Rich led his team to Super Bowl XXXVII in 2003, being the second player from the Delawares to join the Super Bowl. Rich ended his professional career nearly when he surfaced with a serious neck injury in 2004.
