Clark Kellogg | Early Life, Education, and Parents

Clark Clifton Kellogg Jr., professionally Clark Kellogg, was born to Mattie Kellogg, a part-time hospital worker, and Clark Kellogg Sr., a police officer in Cleveland, Ohio, in 1961.

Also, the former NBA player celebrates his birthday on July 2. At the same time, the American-born belongs to the black ethnicity.

Additionally, he enrolled in Chambers Elementary and later transferred to St. Joseph’s High School.

When in High School, Kellogg developed a passion for basketball. With skills for the sport, he received a college grant and subsequently attended Ohio State University.

Broadcasting Career

His career in broadcasting ranges from ESPN, Big East Network, to Prime Sports.

Three years later, CBS offered him a full-time position to cover college games and host March Madness alongside Greg Gumbel and Seth Davis.

Likewise, during coverages, any person would identify Kellogg without seeing him because of his repetitive use of the phrase “spurtability,” referring to the teams that take points in rapid succession.
