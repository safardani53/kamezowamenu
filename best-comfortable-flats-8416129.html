The 13 Most Comfortable Flats of 2024, Tested and Reviewed

Flats are a shoe everyone needs in their wardrobe. With their styling versatility, range of styles, and overall convenience, they are the ultimate everyday shoe that — if constructed well — can provide comfort while running errands, going to work meetings, and even commuting. But, here’s the thing: In order for flats to be worth the investment (and closet space), they have to be comfortable. 

“When selecting any type of shoe style, searching for comfort and support should be the number one priority to maintain good foot function, such as preserving the range of motion of the joints, decreasing foot deformities like bunions and hammertoes, and easing everyday wear and tear to avoid developing blisters, corns, and callus,” Dr. Yolanda Ragland, DPM, a licensed podiatric reconstruction surgeon, tells PEOPLE. She explains that while flat shoes might seem like a comfortable shoe style, many factors make them uncomfortable — particularly the lack of support and traction in some styles. With this in mind, finding flats that provide amble support, traction, and flexibility when walking is important to ensure long-lasting comfort. 

To find the most comfortable flats, the PEOPLE Tested team tried 29 styles across dozens of brands (including Rothy’s, VIVAIA, Birdies, Magauex, and Tory Burch), carefully considering their overall comfort as well as key details like cushion, arch support, and flexibility. Things to Consider Before Buying Comfortable Flats

Materials

When shopping for flats, Dr. Ragland says to look for shoes with durable yet forgiving materials. “Leather and suede are durable, while canvas and fabrics can be more flexible and breathable,” she notes. In addition to comfort, materials can play a major role in your flats' overall care and wear and tear. Machine-washable knit materials are worth considering because they boast durability and hold up well over time (plus, they’re easy to keep fresh between wears). Some knit materials, such as the VIVAIA Pointed-Toe Water Repellent Ballet Flats (Aria 5°), are even water-repellent, so you can wear them in rainy climates without worrying about soaking your soles or ruining your shoes. 

Sole 

The sole is also important to look for, as this can ensure durability, stability, and traction, while also providing some shock absorption, too. Dr. Ragland recommends looking for flats with either a leather, rubber, or cork sole, as these fit the criteria and also hold up against lots of wear and tear. 

Comfort

To state the obvious: Comfortable flats need to be comfortable. And, while some might require a little time to break in, Dr. Ragland says they shouldn’t be uncomfortable from the get-go. “If the shoe hurts in the store, it’s going to hurt later,” she remarks. While nothing compares to trying on a pair of shoes and experiencing the comfort levels for yourself, there are some additional comfort factors to consider (especially when shopping online). First, look for features like padded heels or extra cushion on the balls of the feet. Our top pick for the best everyday flat, the Naturalizer Samantha Half d’Orsay Flat, features this type of cushioning, and we definitely noticed a difference when testing it out. 

It also helps to consider the sizing and opt for a pair that not only fits the length of your foot but also the width. If you have wider feet, consider flats such as the Margaux The Demi, Naturalizer Samantha Half d’Orsay Flat,  Aerosoles Big Bet Ballet Flat, and Birdies The Starling, which come in a wide fit. For more narrow sizes, we also recommend the Naturalizer Samantha Half d’Orsay Flat or the Margaux The Demi. The VIVAIA Square-Toe Mary Jane (Margot Mary Jane) doesn’t technically come in narrow sizes, but we found that it has a more slender fit, so it’s another excellent choice. 

How We Tested 

To find the most comfortable flats, our PEOPLE Tested team researched dozens of styles from top shoe brands before narrowing it down to 29 selections for real-world testing. Over the course of two weeks, the team thoroughly tested each flat by wearing them for a minimum two-hour period each day. While wearing, they carefully noted any changes to their fit, cushion, and comfort with regular wear. 

The team also considered the overall quality and durability of materials, which styles require breaking in, and which ones are most comfortable from the get-go. Additionally, the PEOPLE Tested team made note of any special features like cushioned heels and arch support. Outside of trying them on and walking in them, the PEOPLE Tested team also bent each shoe in half to determine their overall quality and flexibility (which is major in a flat shoe). 

After two weeks of testing and rigorous note-taking, we then compiled all of our insights together and compared each experience to determine which flats were most comfortable. 

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Which designer flats are most comfortable?

    After testing several options, we found that the Tory Burch Minnie Travel Ballet Flats are the most comfortable designer flats. These timeless ballet flats are made from a buttery soft leather material and feature an elastic heel so you can get a more customized fit. On top of that, they’re flexible enough to move with the foot, without losing their support and durability. For another luxe option, we also love Margaux The Demi flats, which come in a variety of colors and feature a classic and timeless ballet flat style that goes with just about anything.

  • Why do my feet hurt in flats?

    Dr. Ragland says flats can sometimes look so deceiving. “Because they are lower to the ground, they give the impression that you are indeed grounded and, therefore, safe,” she notes, explaining that the equivalence of safety to the foot is comfort. “This is not necessarily true when it comes to all flats, as they can cause pain and discomfort equal to their high heel counterparts,” she notes. 

    If you experience heel pain when wearing flats, Dr. Ragland says to first make sure that the flat fits properly and that the width and length are correct for your foot size. “Make sure the toe box is wide and high enough for your toes to have some wiggle room yet snug enough to reduce friction when walking,” she adds. It’s also worth considering your arch height and choosing a flat that accommodates either a flat foot or high arch, depending on where you fall on the spectrum.

  • Should I size up or down in flats?

    While some shoe sizes might vary depending on the brand, Dr. Ragland says it’s important to stay true to size as much as possible. “Sizing up or down is not an option and is a recipe for disaster when it comes to your feet,” she explains. When shopping for a pair of flats, it’s essential to consider any fit notes on the product description page to determine whether or not they run big or small. In these cases, it might be appropriate to size up or down but, for the most part, Dr. Ragland says to avoid this shoe-shopping myth.
