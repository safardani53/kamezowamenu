Vanderpump Rules' Ariana Madix slammed for 'faking' out fans by pretending to move out of $2M LA hom

ARIANA Madix has been facing backlash from fans for pretending to move out of her $2M Los Angeles home.

The Vanderpump Rules star and her cheating ex-boyfriend Tom Sandoval purchased the home in 2019.

On Monday, the 37-year-old posted an Instagram Story video that showed her standing outside her home with stacks of boxes behind her.

She captioned the post: "Ready to dip out," adding a smirking emoji.

Ariana and Tom, 39, called it quits in March after news broke that the Schwartz & Sandy's co-owner cheated with Vanderpump Rules co-star Raquel Leviss.

He and Raquel were thought to be an item for a few months but have since called it quits.

Ariana spoke about continuing to live with Tom, whom she said she has no communication with, during a recent appearance on Watch What Happens Live with Andy Cohen.

The following day she appeared on the Today Show, where she expressed a desire to move out of the home.

She said at the time: "I've been on the phone with a real estate agent.

"And I would like to sell that house and move somewhere, own my own place on my own, and start a new life."

When she posted her Instagram Story about leaving the house, fans were thrilled to her but shortly after the fact it was revealed that it was all a stunt to promote a brand.

Ariana is still living in the home with Tom, according to TMZ.

After her initial post, she made several subsequent videos that confirmed that she was working with a financial company which is why she posted the stories.

She said in the video: "I'm moving up, not out. Well not yet at least.

"It's time to get my financial house in order. I will have more to share with you very very soon."

Ariana also appeared in another video alongside a SoFi employee.

In the caption of the clip, which she shared on her Instagram feed, she wrote: "i’m only surrounding myself with ride-or-dies from now on. SoFi is the all-in-one finance app with a whole squad of financial planners who have YOUR best interests in mind. open an account with @sofi and you can get your own financial bestie to help you make the right decisions with your money, even during the tough times."
