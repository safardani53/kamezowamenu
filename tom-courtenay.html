Tom Courtenay’s Age & Early Life

Tom Courtenay was born in Kingston Upon Hull, East Riding of Yorkshire, England on February 25, 1937. Thomas Daniel Courtenay is his full name. His parents are Annie Eliza and Thomas Henry Courtenay. In addition, he is of British nationality and of Caucasian race.

Moving on to his school history, he attended Kingston High School. Later, he enrolled at University College London to pursue an English degree, but he failed. Tom also attended the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA) in London.

Career

Tom made his film debut as Person on the Street in Without Love (TV Movie). In 1960, he portrayed Bert in Inside Story (TV Series). Similarly, he portrayed Private Potter in ITV Television Playhouse (TV Series) in 1963. In 1970, Courtenay starred as D.H. Lawrence in Solo (TV Series). He portrayed Mark Brian in I Heard the Owl Call My Name (a TV Movie) in 1973. In All the World’s Stage (a TV mini-series), he portrayed the part of Dresser. Tom portrayed Colin in Theatre Night (TV Series) the same year.

Furthermore, in 1995, Tom was cast as Daniel Quilp in The Old Curiosity Shop (TV Movie). He portrayed Roy Southgate in A Rather English Marriage (TV Movie) in 1998. In the 2002 television series Nicholas Nickleby, he portrayed Newman Noggs. He portrayed David Best Sr. in The Royle Family (TV Series) in 2008. Courtenay also played Reggie Paget in Quartet: Deleted Scenes (Video Short) in 2013. In 2015, he portrayed Geoff Mercer in the drama 45 Years. He appeared in many TV movies, including King of Thieves, The Aeronauts, and The Queen’s Corgi. In addition, Tom portrayed Mr. Sullivan in Summerland in 2020. He was cast as Baxter in The North Water (TV Mini-Series) the following year. He appears as Engineer Woodcock in Mandy (TV Series) as of 2022.
