SPEEDWAY star Sam Norris is on a life support machine after suffering serious head injuries from a high-speed crash.

The 15-year-old from Cambridgeshire was riding his 250cc bike in the British Youth Championships at the Peugeot Ashfield Stadium in Glasgow when the accident happened.

Fans on social media have wished the 15-year-old a speedy recovery after the accidentA statement on his official Facebook page read: "Sam is now at paediatric intensive care unit in Glasgow.

"He has a serious brain injury. The staff are observing his condition.

"He's on life support. Thanks to all concerned and please pray for him to get well."

The meeting was abandoned following the horror crash in Heat 23 after his heavy collision with a youngster on the first corner.

An ambulance and paramedic were on the scene within seconds and he was rushed to the hospital.

His mum Claire, who flew to Glasgow on Sunday, added: "Sam is stable at the moment, we don't know how it will go, forever optimistic though.

"Thank you for all your messages and kind words it's overwhelming and supportive too.

"We keep talking to him telling you're all thinking of him. He's made of strong stuff our Super Sam."

Young Lions Speedway tweeted: "Sorry for the slow response to everyone's kind messages, but British Youth 250cc rider Sam Norris is in a serious condition in the Glasgow Hospital after a crash at the Youth Round on Sunday afternoon.
