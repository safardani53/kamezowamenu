How I Met Your Mothers Josh Radnor slams sitcom for stretching 15-minute story into nine f***i

HOW I Met Your Mother’s Josh Radnor has slammed the sitcom for ‘stretching a 15-minute story into nine f***ing years’.

The 45-year-old actor played Ted Mosby in the hit CBS sitcom, which ran from 2005 until 2014.

The sitcom focused on Ted and his life during his 20s and 30s until he met 'The One' - the mother of his two children.

But Josh couldn't help but mock the show in a new Instagram post, holding what looks like a DVD boxset of the series up to the camera.

However the title has been changed to read: "How I Stretched a 15-Minute Story into Nine F***ing Years'.

He captioned the tongue-in-cheek post with: "Well...yeah."

His post comes after Cobie Smulders, who played Robin, defended the show's controversial ending, which revealed Christin Milioti's character Tracy was the kids' mother.

However, Ted revealed she had died six years previously, and went on to ask his children if they would mind him moving on with on-off girlfriend Robin.

Speaking to Metro.co.uk, Cobie said: "‘People are going to feel how they’re going to feel about that. But I think that there’s been enough time.

"Watching it as a streaming show now, you get a little bit more of a connection for other relationships."
