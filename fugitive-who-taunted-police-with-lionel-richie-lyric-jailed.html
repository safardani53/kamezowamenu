Fugitive who taunted police with Lionel Richie lyrics is back behind bars

A prolific burglar who serenaded police with the Lionel Richie song "Hello, is it me you're looking for?" was back behind bars yesterday - All Night Long.

Detectives launched a Facebook hunt for 27-year-old Scott McGiffen begging: "We'd like to hear from anyone who has seen him or knows where he is."

Brazen McGiffen spotted the plea and within an hour later sent back the lyrics of the US singer's schmaltzy 1984 classic.

He wrote after the song's famous opening line: "I can see it in (your) eyes, I can see it in your smile, you're all I ever wanted and my arms are open wide."

McGiffen, whose Facebook profile uses his middle name Perry, taunted them further with crying with laughter emojis and: "Thanks Lionel Richie. Them lyrics fit this post brilliantly."

Pals joined in with one labelling it the "fastest game of hide and seek ever" while another questioned how he would get his drinks round in if he was dangerous and couldn't be approached.

But Ian Dargie wrote: "Wouldn’t it be great to read his posts in court, just after his lawyer has said how sorry he is?"

Hampshire Police - who spelled his name wrong in the Facebook appeal - said yesterday had had been arrested over the November house break-in and had been recalled to prison for breach of licence.
