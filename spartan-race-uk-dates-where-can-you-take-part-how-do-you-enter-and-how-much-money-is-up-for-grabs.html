Spartan Race UK dates: where can you take part, how do you enter and how much money is up for grabs?

THE Spartan race season is returning once again this year, with registrations now open.

It offers open heats for all fitness levels, as well as competitive and elite heats for those with something more to prove.

Spartan Race provides a proving ground for beginner and professional obstacle racers looking to test themselves in new ways.

What is a Spartan Race?

Simply put, the Spartan Race is the world's biggest obstacle course race series - with racers doing a combination of running and obstacles.

Competitors will be taken through forests and eye-catching landscapes as they take on obstacles such as the Spear Throw, Inverted Wall, Monkey Bars and Barbed Wire Crawl.

Where can you take part? 

Races will take place in six different locations across the UK and Ireland between May and October.

The first race weekend of the season kicks off with the South East race weekend on the outskirts of South East London at St Clere on Saturday 4 and Sunday 5 May.

It will then head to Punchestown Racecourse on Saturday 25 and Sunday 26 May.

Most popular

Next up is the Aston Down Airfield in Gloucestershire, on Saturday 22 and Sunday 23 June, and then Spartan returns to the home of Spartan Race UK in the Midlands on Saturday 13 and Sunday 14 July.

Spartan will be then heading to the beautiful Scottish Highlands around Perth for a second year on Saturday 14 and Sunday 15 September.

It will conclude with the season finale, the Windsor Trifecta Weekend, on Saturday 5 and Sunday 6 October - renowned for being the biggest and best race of the season.

Spartan has also launched a new obstacle course event to take place at Twickenham Stadium.

The first UK Stadium Race will be held on Saturday 23 November – the first event of its kind to be run in a major UK sports venue.

Who can take part? 

Each of six race weekends have open heats to cater for racers of all fitness and abilities, Age Group and Elite heats for more competitive racers.

There's also kids races for young adventure thrill seekers aged between 4-13 years old, designed to help inspire them to be more active and get outdoors.

Spartans have four race options to choose from: Sprint, Super, Beast and the Ultra - all of which vary in distance, obstacle count and difficulty.

  • The Sprint puts endurance aside and will test the racers speed over a 5km course.
  • The Super spans 13km and with 25 different obstacles to conquer.
  • The Beast is a demanding 21km circuit across varied terrain with 30 obstacles.
  • The Ultra covers more than 26 miles, and with 60 obstacles to manoeuvre along the way it’s certainly not for the faint-hearted.

    • What's the prize money? 

    This year there is a total prize pot of £17,600 up for grabs, which includes £3,000 for the overall male and female series winners and £600 for the winners of the individual series races so competition looks like it will be fiercer than ever.

    Many athletes from other sports such as CrossFit and traditional trail running are flocking to Obstacle Course Racing to try their hand at both a new challenge and to attempt to win some of that prize pot.

    For more information about Spartan and to register for 2019 events visit www.spartanrace.uk/en
