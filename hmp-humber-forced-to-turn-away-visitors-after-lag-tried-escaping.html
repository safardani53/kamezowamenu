HMP Humber was on lockdown after 'lag tries escaping'

A PRISON was on lockdown after a lag reportedly tried escaping from behind bars.

HMP Humber was forced to turn away visitors after the prisoner tried fleeing today.

The Ministry of Justice has confirmed no one escaped but said there was an incident earlier today.

A prison spokesman said: "HMP Humber has conducted a full roll-check of prisoners following an incident on March 1. All prisoners were accounted for."

Visitors travelling to the prison were left fuming after they were turned away by staff, according to the Hull Daily Mail.

One told the paper: "We were told one prisoner had escaped and another attempted to escaped. They have known for hours and have called us 20 minutes before visiting.

"I have just spent £70 to get here. Everyone is fuming. There's people who have driven hundreds of miles.

"The prison was on lock-down and I was told no one is allowed out and none of them have been fed. I won't be surprised if there's a riot."

The prison is a Category C Resettlement Prison which provides training and education.
