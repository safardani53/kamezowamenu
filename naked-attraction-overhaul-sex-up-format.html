Naked Attraction to get major overhaul as Channel 4 bosses sex up format with spin off show

IT’S the most shocking dating show on TV – and now Naked Attraction could be about to get a spin-off.

The saucy Channel 4 favourite, hosted by Anna Richardson, sees people pick prospective partners by seeing them without their clothes on.

Now, after five years on screen, it’s moving up a level with Naked Attraction Hotel, which has a similar selection process in very different surroundings.

A TV insider said: “The observation producers made was that contestants on the show often ended up wishing they had gone with one of the other choices.

“But in most cases it was too late to get together with them because they had left the process.

“So the idea behind having a hotel is that, after the selection process, all the participants remain in one location and can have dates with multiple people.

"It’s easy to forget with Naked Attraction that the show isn’t just about the section where people’s naked bodies are revealed, it’s also about their actual dating experience once they have put their clothes back on.”

The idea is obviously inspired by Channel 4’s First Dates show, which is fronted by French maitre’d Fred Siriex.

The series began in 2013 and proved such a hit that the spin-off First Dates Hotel followed four years later.

The hotel version is set in the South of France to provide a more romantic setting for the dates.

The original First Dates show was filmed in the City of London, at the Paternoster Chop House, and later at The Refinery restaurant in Manchester.

The new Naked Attraction concept, which has yet to be commissioned, could also tap into the same vibe if the hotel is in a similar fairytale location.
Wherever it is, let’s just hope that it is somewhere warm — for the sake of the poor contestants.

SOS slot nicked by Gabby

NAUGHTY DIY SOS host Nick Knowles will be replaced by glamorous interior designer Gabrielle Blackman in the upcoming Children In Need special.

The regular presenter has to re-cement his relationship with the BBC since getting rapped on the knuckles after making an advert for breakfast cereal Shreddies.

The Sun revealed in May that Nick was close to being sacked from his job of 22 years because making the ad was against the Beeb’s commercial rules.

But he was saved from the chop after apologising and told he could return for the next full series in 2022 once the ad stopped airing.

Gabrielle has been part of the show since last year and is hugely respected in her field, thanks to years in the interiors game.

But can she make a proper builder’s brew? That’s what the crew will want to know.

Graham squirms

CHAT show host and Virgin Radio DJ Graham Norton expected his mum to hate his latest book – because its the first with sex in it.

At first he didn’t even show Home Stretch to her because he felt embarrassed at her reading his take on bedtime activities.

But mum Rhoda Walker simply brushed away his fears about the rude lines.

Graham usually waits until he fully finishes writing before showing anything to her, and he was hesitant this year waiting for her preview notes.

He said: “My mother is a big reader. I feel if she does not like my books, I failed. But this book had some sex in it. So I was like, ‘Mum, I am not sure you are going to like this one’.

“She said: ‘I know about sex.’ I was like, ‘urrgh please stop talking’.”

“Lou organised the meeting because we might get a new kitchen – and now I’ve got eight on the go.”
