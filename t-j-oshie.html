T. J Oshie | Early Life, Family, & Education

Ice Hockey Right Winger T.J Oshie was born on December 23rd, 1986, in Mount Vernon, Washington, to his proud parents Tim Oshie and Tina Oshie. 

His parents raised him in Everett, located in the north of Seattle. He started playing ice hockey at five and was part of the Seattle Junior Hockey Association for around ten years.

After his parent’s divorce, he moved to Stanwood with his mother. Later, he lived with his father and his father’s cousin in Warroad, Minnesota.

Reportedly, T.J belongs to Anishinaabe or Ojibwe Nation, a group of culturally-related inherent people in Canada or the United States. The meaning of his name is in the Ojibwe language, which means – Coming Home.

Moreover, he has three siblings named- Taylor Oshie, Tawni Oshie, and Aleah Hangsleben.

Also, his cousins Gary Sargent and Henry Boucha have both played in the National Hockey League.

According to the horoscope, T.J is a Capricorn. Capricorns are mostly known for their Ambitious, realistic, and persistent nature.

Age, Height, and Body Measurements

Famous Osh turned 36 years old in 2022. He has an athletic build, with a height of 6’1” (185 cm) and 189 lbs weight. Moreover, T.J has dark brown colored hair and beautiful blue eyes.

Education

Furthermore, T.J attended Stanwood High School until his Freshman year, and later, after moving to Warroad, he continued his school years at Warroad High School.

During his days at Warroad High school, for three continuous seasons, he was a star player. He helped lead the team towards Minnesota State Class A titles twice in the years 2003 and 2005. He made it to the State’s All-Tournament Team every single season he played.

T.J won the titles 2005 Associated Press and Pioneer Press All-State First Team, and last but not least, he was Minnesota Mr. Hockey Finalist.

As for his college, Oshie attended the University of North Dakota and played for his alma mater’s ice hockey team.

T.J Oshie | Career – Professional Ice Hockey Athlete

Before jumping into his professional career and achievements, let’s look into his remarkable early playing career.

Early Playing Career

As mentioned above, T.J began his career playing for Seattle Junior Hockey Association. Later proceeded to Warroad, Minnesota, where he was a bantam.

Before his NHL draft, T.J represented the University of North Dakota for three seasons in the Western Collegiate Hockey Association (WCHA).

There Oshie set a school record with nine game-winning goals of the season, which influenced all NCAA players. During the end of his debut season, he was part of the titles WCHA All-Rookie Team and the WCHA Final Five All-Tournament Team.

Moreover, during his sophomore year, T.J scored 52 points, the second rank in team scoring. He acquired Third Team All-WCHA and earned the Fighting Sioux’s Cliff “Fido” Purpur Award, given to the player who expresses hard work and determination while creating excitement on the ice.

Now, let’s hop onto T.J Oshie’s professional Ice Hockey career.

Professional Ice Hockey Career

St. Louis Blues

After playing for his alma mater for three seasons throughout his freshman year to junior year, Oshie chose to forgo his senior season at his college and signed with the NHL team St. Louis Blues on May 13th, 2008.

Washington Capitals

St. Louis Blues traded T.J to the Washington Capitals in exchange for Pheonix Copley, Troy Brouwer, and a third-round draft pick in 2016 on July 2nd, 2015.

He wears number 77 for the Capitals. He had a strong first season with the Capitals and played alongside the Capitals’ top-line Alexander Ovechkin and Nicklas Bäckström.

T.J finished the season with a career-high of 26 goals, which led the team towards winning their second Presidents’ Trophy. He extended his career-high to 33 goals while leading the team towards winning the second consecutive President’s Trophy.

Moreover, T.J signed an eight-year contract worth $46 million with an annual average of $5.75 million to continue playing with the Washinton Capitals on June 23rd, 2017.

During the 2018 Stanley Cup victory, he played a crucial role in the team, scoring eight goals with six on the power-play and 21 points against 24 playoff games.

The Washington Capitals beat the Vegas Golden Knights in Game- 5 of the 2018 Stanley Cup Finals and won their first Stanley Cup in the franchise history on June 7th, 2018.

Oshie is one of the only 43 players to ever have six or more power-play goals throughout a single playoff.

Furthermore, on January 11th, 2020, T.J was part of the 2020 National Hockey League All-Star Game candidates alongside Mitch Marner, Quinn Hughes, and David Perron.

International Career

Apart from the NHL career, T.J has also paved his way into the 2006 World Junior Championships representing the United States. Oshie received his callup for the first senior national team to represent the 2009 IIHF World Championship during the 2009 Stanley Cup playoffs.

He also competed in the 2010 Championship. During the 2012 championship, he was the last addition to the American roster.
