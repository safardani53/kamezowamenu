Career

Dwight began his professional career as a kickboxer. He held the WKA (World Kickboxing Association) middleweight title. Following that, he decided to pursue a career in Mixed Martial Arts (MMA). He began his professional MMA career in 2011 after amassing a 3–1 amateur record.

Furthermore, he primarily fought on the northeastern United States’ provincial circuit. He had an 8-1 record prior to joining the UFC. Similarly, on June 19, 2018, he competed against Tyler Hill at Dana White’s Contender Series 10. He did, however, win the fight by knockout in the second round. This victory earned him a contract with the UFC.

On June 19, 2018, two months after his victory over Hill, he made his promotional debut at UFC on 11-day notice. He was defeated in a tie vote. He was scheduled to fight Chance Renountre, who was filling in for Randy Brown, at UFC Fight Night: Cejudo vs. Dillashaw on January 19, 2019. However, due to an “eye condition,” he was unable to obtain a fight clearance and withdrew from the fight.

He fought Carlo Pedersoli Jr. at UFC Fight Night: Bachowicz vs. Santos on February 23, 2019. He won the first round via technical knockout. Similarly, this victory earned him the Best Performance of the Night award. At UFC 236 on April 13, 2019, he faced Alan Jouban.
