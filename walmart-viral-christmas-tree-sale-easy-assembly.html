You can still get Walmart's viral 'flocked' Christmas tree for $79 that's a dupe for Home Depot's $3

ONE of Walmart's artificial Christmas trees has gone viral and shoppers are rushing to grab it before it sells out.

Customers say it's nearly identical to a tree from The Home Depot that goes for an eyewatering $350.

Holiday Time's flocked Frisco Pine tree measures 6.5 feet tall and is just $79.

The tree is pre-lit with 250 lights that will dazzle throughout the Christmas season.

It has an extra sturdy top branch to support even the biggest and most extravagant tree toppers.

On TikTok, Shalyn (@shalyntyra) was able to snag one of the trees before she even realized it had gone viral.

"I didn't know it was viral until after I left the store and I was researching trees," she said.

"I was like 'Wait, that's the tree I just saw, let me go back and get it before it's out of stock.'"

In the video, she easily assembles the three pieces of the tree into the base and fluffs up the branches.

One user who had previously bought the tree commented: "I've had this for like three years now...it's still going strong."

"I've had this tree for two years now! I love it so much and it's been amazing," added another shopper.

Shalyn said she had seen the Home Depot tree going viral but wasn't sold on the price.

"I would gladly take myself to Walmart and get me a Christmas tree because I'm not paying $350 for a Christmas tree. I just can't," she said.

She was referring to the equally popular flocked tree from Home Depot that comes in multiple sizes.

The Anegeles Home tree measures 7 feet tall but is unlit. The $350 is actually the sale price, as it usually retails for $429.

TRIM THE TREE

Home Depot has another popular, and pricey, option for an artificial tree.

The Grand Duchess Balsam Fir comes in three sizes; 7.5 feet, 9 feet, and 12 feet. The prices are $349, $499, and $899, respectively.

It has gone viral due to its realistic look with over 3,000 branch tips and 2,000 color-changing LED lights.

Also included is a remote that allows users to control different light functions and timers.

Memory wire branches make the tree look full without having to spend time fluffing it once it's assembled.

TikToker Nana Castro (@itsnanacastro) put up her tree on November 1 and described it as “breathtakingly gorgeous."

“Literally, the easiest thing I have ever put together. Each of the bases go into one another and there’s no wiring," she said.  

“I am only five feet tall, I got the nine-foot version so my boyfriend had to help me put up the rest but this thing was like pop and go.”
